This was a simulated phishing exercise conducted by Renewvia to raise awareness about the dangers of phishing. If it had been a real phishing attempt, you would have unknowingly given your personal information to a criminal. The information below can help you recognize phishing messages.

The email was sent from the address nicholas.selby@renewvia.com. However, it is important to note that the sender's email address can easily be forged, and therefore should not be fully trusted.

If you hover your mouse over the link provided in the email, you will see that it would direct you to renewvia-official.github.io, which is not a legitimate Renewvia website. To identify the domain, look for the third forward slash (/) and read the words preceding it. In this case, the domain is github.io. It is also important to note that just because a link has an "https://" and a lock symbol in the browser does not necessarily mean that the website is safe to visit.

To protect yourself from phishing attacks, be cautious of emails and messages that exhibit any of the following six characteristics:

  • A request to click on links
  • An unexpected attachment
  • A sense of urgency
  • An appeal to human greed and/or fear
  • A request for your sensitive data
  • Any non-Renewvia websites asking for your Renewvia account information

Always verify the URL of the website before entering your login credentials. If it is not a Renewvia website, do not provide your credentials. Phishers often direct users to imitation websites that appear legitimate to steal their sensitive information.
