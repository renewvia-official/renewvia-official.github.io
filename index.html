Phishing Awareness Exercise

This was a simulated phishing exercise conducted by Renewvia to raise awareness about the dangers of phishing. If it had been a real phishing attempt, you would have unknowingly given your personal information to a criminal. The information below can help you recognize phishing messages.

What Happened?

You received an email that appeared to be from our CEO. The email contained suspicious elements that should have raised red flags:

  • A sense of urgency in the request
  • Pressure to take immediate action
  • A link to click, even though it was in an email you weren't expecting
  • A slightly altered or suspicious sender email address

You clicked on a link in the email. This could have been either the main "VIEW DOCUMENT" link or the "Click here to report this as a phishing attempt." link in the email footer. Even links that appear to be security-related should not be trusted if they're within a suspicious email. Attackers often include fake security features to build trust.

Our company policy for reporting suspicious emails is to not click any links in the suspicious email, forward the entire email to phishing@renewvia.com, and use Outlook's built-in "Report Phishing" button in the ribbon menu.

To protect yourself from phishing attacks, be cautious of emails and messages that exhibit any of the following six characteristics:

  • A request to click on links or open attachments
  • An unexpected attachment
  • A sense of urgency or pressure
  • Appeals to fear, opportunity, or curiosity
  • Requests for sensitive data or credentials
  • Any non-Renewvia websites asking for your Renewvia account information
  • Unusual requests from executives or colleagues

Always verify the sender's email address carefully and hover over links to see their true destination before clicking. When in doubt, contact the supposed sender through a different channel (phone, Teams, etc.) to verify the request. If it is not a Renewvia website, do not provide your credentials. Phishers often direct users to imitation websites that appear legitimate to steal their sensitive information.
